Combined Resistors.

The spaceship's crew is in serious trouble now since the electronic circuit board for warp-speed-engine-control has blown and they're stalled in outher space.

One resistor on the printed cicuit board has turned into a pile of smoking carbon but luckily someone found in the service documents what value it has to replace it with.

And then, after hours searching thru the box with spare parts over and over, the horrified conclusion is that the requested resistor is not there !!!

However maybe . . . will they be able to make a combination of resistors which then will serve as a replacement and save their souls?

Now it is up to you !

The Mission :

Yyou have to write a piece of code: function "combine(box: tuple, requested: int) -> str" which is given:

the box of the available resistors (as a tuple) and the requested value (as a int) and returns a string describing the searched for combination.

The returned string shall be made up of functions describing how the contents of the box can be connected to achieve the reqested result (which may also represented by just a single number). The combination shall be made entirely of parallel and series connections (for simplicity so called "bridged circuitry" is not needed).

These two functions to do so are: 

"series(*a)" for series connections where "*a" is R1, R2, R3, ....      
"parallel(*a)" for parallel connections where "*a" is R1, R2, R3, ....
(A single resistor can be represented by its value alone.)

A few examples : 

combine((3, 3, 6), 5) == "series(3, parallel(3, 6))"

combine((20, 20, 30, 30), 24) == "series(parallel(20, 30), parallel(20, 30))"
                              or "parallel(series(20, 20), series(30, 30))"

combine((12, 12, 12, 12), 3) == "parallel(12, 12, 12, 12)"
                             or "parallel(parallel(12, 12), parallel(12, 12))"

combine((24, 12), 12) == "12"   # (One single resistor.)

combine((6, 9), 12) == "None"   # (Oh no, can't be done!)

The on the mission involved mathematics :

You can calculate the total resistance of two resistors (R1, R2) series connected (Rs) by:

Rs = R1 + R2

And the calculation for two resistors (R1, R2) parallel connected (Rp) is:

Rp = 1 / (1 / R1 + 1 / R2) Which can also be written as : Rp = R1 * R2 / (R1 + R2)
